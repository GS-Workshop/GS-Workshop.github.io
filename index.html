GSW 2023: The 2nd Great Shit Workshop

Call for Great Shits

2022 is not easy for us. However, we should be proud that we are still progressing in such a challenging period. At the end of this year, it is a good time for us to revisit what we achieved in a relaxing, funny way. This workshop aims to bring together undergraduate and graduate students majoring in computer science, artificial intelligence, statistics, and other relevant subjects to share interesting ideas and personal writing experiences. We, especially, invite submissions that can be defined as "Great Shits" to some degree. Ideally, a great shit should satisfy the following three conditions: (1) it was started from an interesting or significant idea; (2) its first draft was so terrible as a piece of shit; and (3) its final draft becomes awesome (or at least, tolerable) after thorough polishment.

If you have any "great shit" work created in 2022 (e.g., paper, homework, technical report, and so on), feel free to send it to gsworkshop@126.com. Each submission must be your own work and should include the first draft and the corresponding final version. For each submission, pdf format is preferred, but there is no restriction on anonymity, length, or language. A committee will review all submissions and nominate one as the "Annual Greatest Shit". The evaluation criteria include (1) the solidness of the idea and (2) the improvements in writing. The authors of the "Annual Greatest Shit" will receive a secret prize:) Whether a virtual workshop will be held or not depends on the submission status.
